The Opportunity

The problem
isn't supply.
It's coordination.

There are more than enough parking spaces in every major American city. The problem is that drivers can't find them, can't reserve them in advance, and can't access them seamlessly when they arrive.

Every other transportation category — flights, hotels, rideshare — solved this coordination problem. Parking hasn't. The technology to fix it exists today. What hasn't existed is a platform built to own the full experience.

Parkt is that platform.

The fragmentation problem
The average driver in a major U.S. city manages 4+ separate parking apps, none of which provide real-time availability, advance reservation, and seamless gate access in a single experience. The result is wasted time, wasted fuel, and billions of dollars in friction annually.
The digitization gap
The U.S. parking market generates over $130B in annual revenue yet remains one of the least digitized consumer categories in the economy. Most transactions still involve a human attendant, a cash payment, or a broken kiosk. The infrastructure is ready for a technology layer — it just doesn't exist yet.
The timing
Mobile payment adoption has crossed the threshold that makes a parking platform viable at scale. GPS accuracy, computer vision for plate recognition, and real-time inventory APIs are all mature and affordable. The window to build the category-defining platform is open right now.
The ESG dimension
930 million gallons of fuel burn every year from drivers circling for parking — one of the most avoidable sources of urban vehicle emissions. Every reservation made through Parkt eliminates a search loop. At scale, this is a measurable, auditable environmental outcome that institutional investors and corporate partners increasingly require.
Business Model

Multiple revenue streams.
All additive.

Parkt's model is designed to grow margin over time as the platform scales from a direct marketplace to a full ecosystem. Each stage builds on the one before it.

STREAM 01
Transaction Revenue
Parkt earns revenue on every parking transaction processed through the platform. Drivers pay once. Parkt manages the economics on both sides.
STREAM 02
Operator Platform
Parking operators and property managers access Parkt's management tools and analytics on a recurring basis. Sticky, scalable, and high-margin as the operator network grows.
STREAM 03
Platform Services
Additional revenue streams emerge naturally as platform scale creates new value for both operators and drivers. The full model is detailed in the investor deck.

The Founder
GK
Grant M. Kolb
Founder & CEO
getparkt.com
Grant Kolb built Parkt because he couldn’t stop thinking about a problem he watched repeat itself in every city he traveled to for work. Airports, stadiums, city centers across the country — the parking experience was identical everywhere: multiple apps, no real-time information, no advance reservation, and a transaction that still required a human at a booth. The infrastructure existed. The coordination layer did not.
Rather than wait for someone else to build it, he built it himself — completing the full business plan, product architecture, legal framework, brand system, and go-to-market strategy before raising a dollar of external capital. Six years as an early employee at a fast-growing reinsurance brokerage taught him how to price risk, build relationships in complex markets, and scale a business without a blueprint. Those skills transfer directly to building a marketplace.
He didn’t build Parkt because parking is a good market. He built it because the problem is real, the solution is achievable, and nobody has done it right yet. Minneapolis-based, Minnesota-focused to start, with a clear national expansion thesis. The beachhead market is the city he knows best. The ambition is every parking market in the country.
